Bangkok fire investigation finds locked doors and flammable decor as deaths climb to 30

In the aftermath of a devastating fire in Bangkok, investigators have reported harrowing findings, as the death toll climbs to 30. The probe into the tragedy has revealed that several doors within the affected building were locked, significantly hampering escape efforts. According to first responders and survivors, these locked exits created a perilous environment, leaving many individuals trapped and unable to flee the advancing flames. Further compounding the danger was the lack of signage marking emergency exits, which added confusion and delay for those attempting to escape. In addition to these critical failures, the building's interiors were adorned with flammable decor, which likely accelerated the spread of the fire, contributing to its lethality. Investigations are ongoing, and authorities are determined to uncover the complete sequence of events that led to this catastrophe. The incident has sparked urgent calls for stricter enforcement of building safety regulations, aiming to prevent similar occurrences in the future. In the wake of the disaster, the focus is now on supporting the families affected by the tragic loss of life and ensuring that necessary safety improvements are implemented across the city to safeguard the public.
