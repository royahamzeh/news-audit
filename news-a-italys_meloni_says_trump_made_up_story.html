Italy's Meloni says Trump 'made up' story that she 'begged' him for photo at G7

Italy's Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni has publicly refuted claims made by former U.S. President Donald Trump that she 'begged' him for a photograph during a G7 summit. This situation arises amid increasing tensions between the two, highlighting a shift from their previously cordial relationship. According to Meloni, Trump has fabricated the story, possibly to wield influence in his ongoing political endeavors. The allegations surfaced after Trump's controversial escalation with Iran following his re-entry into political campaigning, which has evidently strained many of his international relationships, including that with Meloni. Analysts suggest that the fraying ties may have wider implications, possibly affecting bilateral NATO discussions as well as economic accords involving Italy and the United States. The public spat underscores a broader geopolitical shift in Europe, where leadership is re-evaluating alliances in a rapidly changing global landscape. Meloni emphasized that while she acknowledges past discussions with Trump, the current circumstances demand a focus on global diplomacy and stability over personal political narratives. Observers posit that this incident may reflect Meloni’s attempt to position her leadership more independently on the world stage, distancing herself from Trump amid his contentious foreign policy decisions.
