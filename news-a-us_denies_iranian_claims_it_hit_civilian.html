US denies Iranian claims it hit civilian infrastructure in latest strikes

The United States has refuted claims by Iran suggesting that recent US military strikes targeted civilian infrastructure. According to the US Department of Defense, the strikes were specifically directed to undermine Iranian military capabilities. Tensions between the two nations have been escalating, and this recent exchange further strains diplomatic relations. The US military insisted that the strikes were precise and aimed at military targets associated with Iran's operations in the region. On the other hand, Iranian officials have accused the US of recklessness and of targeting civilian areas, a claim that has been categorically denied by US sources. These strikes are part of a broader strategy by the United States to limit Iran's influence and military prowess in the Middle East, attributing frequent provocations and hostile activities to Iranian forces. The Pentagon highlighted that all measures were taken to avoid civilian areas, emphasizing their commitment to minimizing collateral damage in conflict zones. The US reiterates its position that these actions were necessary to deter further aggression from Iran, pointing out that maintaining regional stability requires decisive action against threats posed by Iranian military forces. The Iranian government, however, remains adamant about its version of events, further complicating ongoing diplomatic efforts and regional security dynamics.
