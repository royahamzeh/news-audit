Italian officials handed jail terms for Genoa bridge disaster that killed 43

Genoa Bridge Disaster: Justice Served

The tragic collapse of the Morandi Bridge in Genoa in 2018 led to the deaths of 43 people. After lengthy legal proceedings, significant accountability has been established.

Key Sentences

  • Giovanni Castellucci, ex-head of Autostrade per l’Italia, was sentenced to 12 years.
  • Executives faced charges including manslaughter and safety violations.

Investigation Findings

  • Ignored maintenance procedures.
  • Profit prioritized over safety.

Public Reaction

Families of the victims and the Italian public have welcomed the verdict, viewing it as a landmark decision in corporate accountability.

Conclusion

While the sentences bring some closure, it raises questions on infrastructure oversight amidst a network of aging bridges and roads in Italy.
