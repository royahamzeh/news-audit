Two boys pulled from Venezuela earthquake rubble among 33 people rescued over weekend

In the wake of the devastating earthquake that struck Venezuela, the country is engulfed in despair as tens of thousands remain missing. Over the weekend, a sliver of hope emerged as 33 people, including two young boys, were pulled from the rubble by rescue teams. These rescues have provided glimmers of relief to desperate families, but the overarching reality remains grim as time runs out to find more survivors. The urgency has never been greater, as the affected communities face the potential of losing any chance to reunite with their loved ones still trapped underneath the debris. Rescue teams are working relentlessly through the debris, deploying all available resources and technology to locate trapped individuals. However, with each passing hour, the reality sets in that the window of opportunity to find additional survivors is rapidly closing. Families linger near effaced neighborhoods, waiting for any word or sign about their missing relatives. The anguish, compounded by gaps in resources and communication, amplifies the challenges for both the families and the rescuers. As night falls again on devastated landscapes, it becomes another waiting game for families who hold onto hope, enduring the emotional toll of uncertainty while rescue efforts continue in the face of decreasing odds of finding more survivors.
