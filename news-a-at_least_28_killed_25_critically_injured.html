At least 28 killed, 25 critically injured after fire engulfs Bangkok bar

A devastating fire broke out at a bustling bar in Bangkok’s Chatuchak district, leading to a tragic death toll of at least 28 people and critically injuring 25 others. The horrific incident unfolded late in the evening, engulfing the popular entertainment venue in flames. As firefighters rushed to the scene, they were met with the chaotic sight of patrons desperately trying to flee through the fiery inferno. The blaze quickly spread throughout the bar, with thick plumes of smoke and intense heat complicating rescue efforts. Emergency responders worked tirelessly to control the blaze and evacuate the remaining patrons, some of whom sustained severe burns and smoke inhalation injuries. Eyewitnesses reported hearing cries for help amidst the chaos, with many people trapped inside due to blocked exits and dense crowds. The authorities have launched an investigation to determine the cause of the fire, amid concerns about safety standards and fire code compliance. As the community reels from this heartbreaking loss, families and friends of the victims are grappling with the sudden and violent disruption of their lives. The incident highlights the urgent need for improved safety measures in entertainment venues to prevent such tragedies from occurring in the future.
