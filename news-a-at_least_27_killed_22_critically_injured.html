At least 27 killed, 22 critically injured after fire engulfs Bangkok bar

A tragic fire broke out at a bar in the Chatuchak district of Bangkok, leaving at least 27 people dead and 22 critically injured. As the fire engulfed the establishment, patrons were seen running through the flames in a desperate attempt to escape. The horrific incident occurred late in the evening, drawing a swift response from local emergency services. Firefighters arrived on the scene to find the bar engulfed in flames, with smoke billowing into the night sky. It took several hours for the fire to be brought under control. Fire investigators are currently examining the site to determine the cause of the blaze, while authorities have launched an investigation into whether the establishment adhered to safety regulations. Witnesses describe the scene as chaotic, with people trapped inside the burning building and others attempting to rescue them. The injured were rushed to nearby hospitals, where some remain in critical condition. This tragedy has highlighted potential issues with the safety infrastructure and emergency preparedness of entertainment venues in the city. Local authorities have promised a thorough review of such establishments to prevent future disasters. Meanwhile, condolences pour in from around the world, with many expressing sympathy and solidarity with the victims' families during this difficult time.
