Power outages hit France as record heatwave set to peak

France is currently grappling with severe power outages in the Brittany region as a record-breaking heatwave blankets the country. Approximately 68,000 homes are left without electricity, disrupting daily activities and raising concerns over the vulnerabilities of the national grid amidst extreme weather conditions. The outages come at a time when temperatures have soared to unprecedented levels, intensifying the strain on electricity networks as households and businesses ramp up their use of fans and air-conditioning units in a bid to stay cool. Energy companies are working tirelessly to address the issue, but they have cautioned that full restoration of electricity might not be possible until Wednesday night. The Brittany region, known for its typically mild climate, is facing significant challenges as it contends with the combination of soaring temperatures and power failures. Local authorities are urging residents to take precautions against the heat and to expect further disruptions in services. Emergency measures are being implemented to provide assistance to those most affected by the outages, including the elderly and individuals with medical needs. This situation underscores the urgent necessity for France to bolster its infrastructure resilience in the face of increasingly frequent and severe weather events linked to climate change.
