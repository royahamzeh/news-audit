Huge crowds in Mashhad as Iran's late supreme leader is buried

Iran Mourns Late Supreme Leader

Ali Khamenei, Iran's late Supreme Leader, was laid to rest in Mashhad at the Imam Reza shrine, marking the end of six days of public mourning ceremonies. The shrine, esteemed as Iran's holiest Shia Muslim site, became the gathering point for immense crowds mourning his passing.

Key Moments

  • Khamenei's burial at the revered shrine.
  • Six days of national mourning ceremonies.
  • Massive crowds from across Iran and beyond.

Legacy Reflection

The burial signifies more than the end of an era; it is a time for reflection on Khamenei’s complex legacy. Revered by many, his leadership was pivotal in navigating Iran through critical periods of internal and international tension. The end of mourning raises significant inquiries regarding Iran’s future political trajectory.
