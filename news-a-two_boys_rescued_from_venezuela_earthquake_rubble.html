Two boys rescued from Venezuela earthquake rubble after days of being trapped

In a harrowing tale of survival, two young boys have been rescued from the rubble of buildings that collapsed following a devastating earthquake in Venezuela. The earthquake, which struck several days ago, has left many buildings in ruins and communities in turmoil. Rescuers worked tirelessly for six hours to dig through the debris carefully and reach one of the boys. Using their hands and limited tools available, they inched their way through the rubble, driven by the hope of finding survivors. Although some heavy machinery is expected to arrive, the current reality is many residents have been forced to rely on manual labor, desperately digging through bricks and broken wood in an attempt to find remaining survivors. As urgency mounts, tales of miraculous rescues amidst the tragedy provide a glimmer of hope. In the face of adversity, the community’s resilience shines through as residents work together in a race against time. With many still missing, hopes remain pinned on the incoming machinery, which promises to expedite the rescue process. This devastating event has sparked international sympathy, and emergency aid is being mobilized worldwide to assist Venezuela during this challenging time and provide relief to its affected citizens.
