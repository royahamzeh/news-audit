Israel carries out air strikes on Lebanon, state media says, as Iran claims deal with US near

Israel has conducted air strikes on targets in Lebanon, marking yet another escalation in regional hostilities, as reported by state media. This development comes amid remarks from Iran's foreign minister, who stated that a deal with the United States aimed at ending the ongoing conflict is close to fruition. The air strikes in Lebanon are viewed as part of a broader geopolitical struggle involving Israel, Iran, and the United States, with Lebanon positioned precariously in the middle. Israeli officials have not released specific details about the targets or the outcomes of the strikes, leaving analysts to speculate on the potential implications for regional stability. The Iranian foreign minister's comments concerning an imminent deal with the US add a layer of complexity, as it suggests parallel diplomatic channels are being engaged even as military actions unfold. The international community remains on edge, with fears that continued military engagements could obstruct any diplomatic progress. Observers are closely monitoring these developments, hoping that peace negotiations between Iran and the US might proceed uninhibited, ultimately contributing to regional de-escalation. However, the impact of Israel's military actions on these diplomatic efforts remains uncertain, adding tension to an already volatile situation.
