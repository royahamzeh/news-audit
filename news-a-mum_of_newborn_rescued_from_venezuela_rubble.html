Mum of newborn rescued from Venezuela rubble says baby motivated her to stay alive

In the aftermath of the devastating earthquakes in Venezuela, rays of hope have emerged with the miraculous rescue of 18-day-old baby Juan David. Amidst the chaos, Juan’s mother shared her harrowing experience of being trapped under the rubble, explaining that the thought of her newborn son was the only thing that kept her going. The tragedy has left entire communities shattered, with families searching for loved ones and efforts underway to provide emergency aid. Yet, amidst the destruction, the story of Juan’s rescue offers a glimmer of hope for the survivors and the rescuers who have been working tirelessly. Both mother and child were trapped in what was once their home. Despite being enveloped in despair, the mother’s maternal instincts kicked in, and she clung to life, calling out until rescuers heard her cries. This incredible story has disseminated a message of resilience, becoming a beacon of hope and motivation for those affected by the tragedy. The rescue team’s determination alongside the mother’s resolute spirit serves as a testament to human endurance. As the country comes together to rebuild, they do so inspired by Juan’s story, which serves as a reminder of the preciousness of life and the miracles that can emerge from despair.
