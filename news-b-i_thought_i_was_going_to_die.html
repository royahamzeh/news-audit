'I thought I was going to die' - Venezuelans describe earthquake panic

Venezuelans Describe Earthquake Panic

In a frightening turn of events, Venezuela's capital was rocked by two successive earthquakes, the first measuring 7.2 in magnitude followed by a stronger 7.5 quake.

  • Residents experienced fear and chaos as buildings swayed and debris fell.
  • The city's infrastructure faced considerable challenges under the dual seismic tremors.
  • Survivors share harrowing experiences, one stating, 'I thought I was going to die.'
  • Emergency services were on high alert, working tirelessly despite barriers.
  • The nation's ongoing economic & political issues compound emotional trauma.

Community and Assistance

Despite the overwhelming situation, stories of bravery and unity emerged from the communities most affected.

  • Neighbors and strangers supported each other, showing immense resilience.
  • Authorities continue intensive rescue and recovery efforts.
  • Experts caution about possible aftershocks, advising vigilance.
