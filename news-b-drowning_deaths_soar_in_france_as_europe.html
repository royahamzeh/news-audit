Drowning Deaths Soar in France Amidst Heatwave

France is grappling with a surge in drowning deaths as an intense heatwave wreaks havoc across Europe. Since last Thursday, 40 people have succumbed to heatwave-related drowning, according to Prime Minister Sébastien Lecornu. The relentless rise in temperatures has driven people to water bodies in droves, seeking relief but often resulting in overcrowded conditions and tragic accidents. Efforts are underway to bolster safety protocols and swiftly disseminate crucial information to communities.
