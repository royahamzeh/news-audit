Saudis must recognise Israel for nuclear deal, says Trump

Former US President Donald Trump has announced that for Saudi Arabia to receive assistance in developing nuclear energy, the Kingdom must first normalize relations with Israel by joining the Abraham Accords. This announcement marks a significant condition in what is being dubbed as a landmark agreement, one that could reshape geopolitical alliances and energy dynamics in the Middle East. Trump's stance insists that Saudi Arabia follow the lead of several other Arab nations that made peace with Israel under the Accords, an initiative Trump himself championed during his presidency. The agreement, if reached, promises to open new avenues for energy collaboration while potentially serving as a deterrent against nuclear proliferation intentions adversaries may harbor in the region. Trump's preconditions also align with broader objectives of strengthening Israeli security and further integrating Israel into the Middle Eastern political landscape. Critics of the agreement voice concerns over nuclear technology proliferation and the potential for regional armament, arguing that this could enhance military tensions. Nonetheless, proponents argue that enhanced diplomatic relations could foster regional stability. With Trump's condition now public, discussions surrounding the proposed nuclear energy collaboration are expected to intensify, with keen global attention on how Saudi-Israeli relations may evolve as negotiations progress.
