Zelensky to press Nato for air defence systems after intense Russian strikes

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky is set to attend a pivotal NATO meeting in Turkey, where he will press Western allies for enhanced air defense systems. The meeting comes in the wake of intensified Russian air strikes that have targeted key infrastructure across Ukraine. Zelensky's primary focus will be to secure a commitment for more advanced interceptor missiles to bolster Ukraine's defense capabilities. With the ongoing conflict showing no signs of abatement, the Ukrainian leader is expected to emphasize how crucial these systems are for protecting civilian lives and maintaining national sovereignty. The plea for additional support highlights the escalating stakes as Ukraine tries to fend off a relentless barrage of attacks from Russian forces. Zelensky's presence at the NATO summit underscores the urgency of the situation and the need for international solidarity and support. While NATO members have provided substantial aid in various forms, Zelensky is likely to stress that Ukraine’s current arsenal falls short of effectively countering the sophisticated weaponry deployed by Russia. The outcome of these discussions could have significant implications for the dynamics of the conflict, as well as for NATO's role in supporting its non-member allies.
