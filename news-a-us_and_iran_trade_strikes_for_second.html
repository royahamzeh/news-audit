US and Iran trade strikes for second night in a row after Trump declares ceasefire 'over'

Tensions between the United States and Iran have escalated once again, as both nations engaged in military strikes for a second consecutive night, following former President Trump's declaration that the previous ceasefire was 'over.' The resurgence of hostilities has led to a dramatic decrease in traffic through the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global oil transportation. The latest attacks have heightened fears of potential disruptions in the global oil supply and have raised concerns about the broader implications for regional stability. The Strait of Hormuz sees about 20% of the world's oil shipments, and any disruptions in this area could have significant repercussions on global energy prices. Analysts suggest that the recent actions may lead to further economic instability in the region and beyond. The events come after a period of relative calm, following the ceasefire agreement that had brought temporary relief to the strife-torn area. The international community is closely monitoring the situation, as diplomatic efforts to de-escalate tensions have yet to show significant progress. With the recent developments, the prospect of a swift resolution appears more uncertain, raising the stakes for future diplomatic negotiations.
