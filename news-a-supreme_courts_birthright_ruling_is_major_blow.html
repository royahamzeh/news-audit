Supreme Court's birthright ruling is major blow to Trump

The US Supreme Court has delivered a significant legal blow to President Donald Trump with its recent ruling on birthright citizenship. The court's decision reaffirms the constitutional right recognized under the 14th Amendment, which grants citizenship to anyone born on US soil. This verdict undermines Trump's long-standing agenda to end birthright citizenship, a key component of his immigration policy. The ruling carries profound implications for the president's legislative framework and provides a check on executive power. It ensures that millions of children born to immigrant families in the US retain their citizenship status, offering a degree of stability amidst the administration's broader attempts to overhaul the immigration system. The decision highlights a pivotal moment in the tension between the judiciary and the executive branch of government. Analysts suggest that the ruling could galvanize Trump's opponents and immigrant rights advocates. By securing the rights of those born in the US, regardless of their parents' nationality, the Supreme Court has maintained a long-standing constitutional interpretation. This move fundamentally challenges Trump's rhetoric and policy goals, posing a significant setback to his administration's efforts to redefine American citizenship. As the country approaches election season, this legal ruling is expected to play a crucial role in the ongoing political discourse.
