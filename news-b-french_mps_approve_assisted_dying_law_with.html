French MPs Approve Assisted Dying Law

The French parliament has passed a landmark bill allowing assisted dying for terminally ill adults under strict conditions, marking a significant shift in the country's end-of-life care policy.

Key Points

  • The law permits assisted dying for adults with terminal illnesses experiencing unbearable suffering.
  • Strict criteria require repeated confirmation of consent and comprehensive medical consultations.
  • Critics express concerns over ethical and societal impacts, while proponents advocate for personal choice and dignity.
