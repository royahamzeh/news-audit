At least 13 killed in large-scale Russian missile and drone strikes on Kyiv

In a tragic escalation of violence, at least 13 people have been killed in a series of Russian missile and drone strikes on Kyiv. Ukrainian officials state that this marks the largest assault on the city in recent weeks, with children among the numerous casualties. The strikes have exacerbated an already dire humanitarian situation, leading to widespread fear and instability among residents. Ukrainian emergency services were quick to respond, working tirelessly to rescue those trapped in the rubble and administer medical aid to the wounded. Local infrastructure has suffered significant damage, with some areas facing power shortages and disruptions in communication. Eyewitness accounts depict a scene of chaos and destruction as many buildings have been reduced to debris. The Ukrainian government has condemned the attacks, calling for immediate international intervention and urging allied nations to strengthen sanctions against Russia. Nations worldwide have expressed their outrage and empathy, with many pledging further support for Ukraine. As the conflict intensifies, the global community remains on high alert, fearing broader implications of the ongoing hostilities. The continued assault on civilians highlights the urgent need for diplomatic efforts to de-escalate tensions and pursue lasting peace in the region.
