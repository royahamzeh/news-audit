Iran and Israel say they have halted strikes after first exchange of fire since truce

Tensions have escalated sharply in the Middle East following the first exchange of fire between Iran and Israel since a previously established truce. The confrontation began when Iran launched approximately 30 missiles targeting Israeli territory. This came as retaliation after an Israeli airstrike in Lebanon, which Israel attributed to Iranian-backed forces operating in the region. In response to Iran's missile launches, Israel conducted two waves of airstrikes targeting facilities in Iran. This latest escalation has raised alarms across the international community as it threatens to destabilize the delicate balance achieved by the truce. Diplomatic channels have been working intensively, with various countries urging both Iran and Israel to exercise restraint and avoid further military engagements. This exchange underscores the fragile nature of peace in the region and the potential for rapidly escalating conflicts. Following these military actions, both nations have announced a cessation of strikes, a move that many hope will pave the way for renewed dialogue and de-escalation. Observers note that maintaining open communication lines and addressing the root causes of tensions are critical to preventing further escalation.
