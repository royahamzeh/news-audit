Tehran selling deal with US as victory – but for Iranians it was necessity

The recent deal between Tehran and the United States is being hailed by Iranian officials as a diplomatic victory, a testament to their skill in negotiating with one of their longest-standing adversaries. However, for many ordinary Iranians, the situation is a bit more nuanced. They view the agreement not through the lens of political triumph but as a crucial step towards alleviating the everyday challenges they face. High inflation and international sanctions have crippled the Iranian economy, leading to soaring prices of basic goods and services. This economic pressure, compounded by the perpetual fear of potential military conflict, has left the populace yearning for stability. The deal, therefore, is seen less as an achievement of diplomatic prowess and more as a necessity, a lifeline that they hope will stabilize their economy and allow them to live without the looming fear of war. The true measure of the deal's success, for the average Iranian, will be its ability to bring prices down and provide a sense of security. While officials might project this as a strategic win on the global chessboard, the tangible benefits at home will ultimately define its worth for the people of Iran.
