Zelensky's removal of popular defence minister sparks protests in Ukraine

In a surprising move, Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky has dismissed Defense Minister Viktor Fedorov, prompting significant protests across the nation. The dismissal appears to stem from increasing tensions and a reported rift between Fedorov and the Commander-in-Chief of the Armed Forces, Oleksandr Syrskyi. This development has sparked widespread concern about the stability and unity within Ukraine's defense forces at a critical time. Fedorov was considered a popular figure, both within the military and among the public, due to his outspoken stance on military reforms and his role in modernizing the armed forces. His removal has therefore come as a shock to many, resulting in protests from those who view him as an essential figure in Ukraine's ongoing conflict with separatist forces in the east. Political analysts suggest that this decision may reflect deeper strategic disagreements within Ukraine's military hierarchy, which President Zelensky felt were untenable. The timing of this move is also noteworthy, as it comes amid growing international scrutiny over Ukraine's defense strategies and potential shifts in its military alliances. As protests continue, it remains to be seen how this decision will impact Zelensky's administration and Ukraine’s overall defense strategy in the months ahead.
