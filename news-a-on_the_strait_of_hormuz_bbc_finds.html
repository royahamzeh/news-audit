On the Strait of Hormuz, BBC finds seized ships and shark fishermen as uneasy calm returns

Bandar Abbas, a key port city located near the strategic Strait of Hormuz, has long been influenced by geopolitical tensions. In the wake of the recent US-Israeli conflict with Iran, the city has found a fragile tranquility returning to its shores. The BBC's visit to Bandar Abbas reveals a city adapting to the turbulent shifts in international dynamics. The strait, a vital oil transportation route, witnessed disruptions as ships were seized amid heightened tensions. These incidents not only affected global oil markets but also added stress to the local economy heavily reliant on maritime activities. Despite these challenges, daily life in Bandar Abbas is finding a semblance of normalcy. Fishermen, whose livelihoods depend on the rich marine biodiversity of the strait, have resumed their operations, though with caution. Shark fishing, a traditional occupation, has continued as residents balance economic survival with safety concerns. Authorities are wary, but optimistic, about maintaining stability. While the city's residents hope for lasting peace, they remain cautious, understanding the potential volatility inherent in the region's geopolitics. As Bandar Abbas oscillates between calm and conflict, its people display resilience, determined to navigate the complexities imposed by the global political landscape.
