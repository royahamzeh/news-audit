'Strongest quake I've ever felt' - Venezuelans describe panic

Venezuela was struck by two powerful earthquakes that shook the capital, causing widespread panic among residents. The initial quake registered a magnitude of 7.2, followed by an even stronger tremor measuring 7.5 just seconds later. The events triggered immediate fear as buildings swayed and citizens scrambled for safety, echoing the sentiments of residents who described it as the strongest seismic activity they had ever experienced. In the aftermath, communication networks were disrupted, and emergency services were quickly deployed to assess the scale of damage and provide assistance. Despite the intense fear, preliminary reports indicate minimal casualties, though structural damages are expected to strain resources further in the coming days. This seismic event highlights Venezuela’s vulnerability to natural disasters and the necessity for disaster preparedness measures in this region, which sits on the edge of a tectonic plate. Residents described the atmosphere as terrifying, with many caught off guard by the sudden and severe shaking, running into the streets with a sense of urgency. While recovery efforts are underway, the dual quakes have left a lasting impression, reminding citizens of the unpredictable nature of living in an active seismic zone.
