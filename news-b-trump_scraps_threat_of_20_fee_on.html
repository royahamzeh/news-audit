Trump scraps threat of 20% fee on Hormuz cargo as US resumes blockade of Iran ports

Trump Retracts Hormuz Cargo Fee Threat

In a surprising decision, former President Donald Trump has withdrawn his recently announced plan to impose a 20% fee on cargo ships passing through the strategic Strait of Hormuz. This comes just 24 hours after the original declaration.

  • The fee was part of a broader U.S. strategy against Iran's control over the passage.
  • Quick backlash came from shipping industries and international stakeholders.
  • Despite retracting the fee, the U.S. has renewed its blockade of Iranian ports.

Continuing the Blockade Strategy

While retracting the fee threat, the U.S. maintains pressure on Iran with its resumed blockade. This is aimed at isolating Iran economically and politically but risks potential escalation between nations involved.
