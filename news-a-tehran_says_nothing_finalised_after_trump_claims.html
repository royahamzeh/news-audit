Tehran says 'nothing' finalised after Trump claims deal to end Iran war near

Tehran has dismissed claims made by US President Donald Trump that a 'great settlement' has been reached to end the ongoing conflict involving Iran. A spokesperson for the Iranian Foreign Ministry stated that reports of such an agreement are 'speculative' and emphasized that 'nothing' has been finalized. This development follows President Trump's assertion that a deal was imminent, fueling speculation and raising hopes of a resolution. Iran remains cautious but continues to engage diplomatically with other international actors. The spokesperson reiterated Iran's commitment to peaceful negotiations but stressed that concrete progress was necessary before confirming any agreement. Analysts suggest that while diplomatic channels between Washington and Tehran remain open, the complexities of regional politics and existing sanctions complicate the peace process. Observers also point out that Trump's announcement could be a strategic move to gauge responses from various stakeholders. As tensions persist, there is consensus among experts that any significant breakthroughs will require substantial concessions from all parties involved. For now, the Iranian position remains firm in underlining that no official conclusion has been reached. This development illustrates the ongoing diplomatic balancing act where both sides cautiously navigate towards a potential peaceful settlement while skepticism persists.
