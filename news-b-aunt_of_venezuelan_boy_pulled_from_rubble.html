Aunt of Venezuelan boy pulled from rubble tells BBC she will give him 'mother's warmth'

Venezuelan Boy Rescued from Earthquake Rubble

In a dramatic and hopeful rescue, Kleiber Moran, a 2-year-old boy from Venezuela, was freed from the rubble on Wednesday. This remarkable event occurred six days after a devastating earthquake struck the region, leaving behind a trail of destruction and chaos. Rescue operations have been ongoing, with this successful rescue bringing a glimmer of hope to many affected by the disaster.

Aunt’s Heartwarming Pledge

Speaking to the BBC, Kleiber's aunt shared her profound relief and joy at her nephew's survival. With determination, she expressed her commitment to providing a nurturing environment akin to a 'mother's warmth' to help him overcome the trauma. This emphasis on love and care highlights the importance of family in the healing process.
