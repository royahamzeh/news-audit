At least 10 killed in large-scale Russian missile and drone strikes on Kyiv

In a grim reminder of the ongoing conflict in Eastern Europe, at least ten people have been reported dead following one of the largest missile and drone strikes on Kyiv in recent weeks. Ukrainian officials confirmed that among the casualties are children, highlighting the indiscriminate nature of the assault. The strikes struck multiple targets across the city, causing widespread destruction and a significant number of injuries. This escalation marks a severe uptick in hostilities, and the international community is once again calling for restraint and a cessation of violence. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky condemned the attacks, urging the global community to increase pressure on Moscow to de-escalate tensions. He emphasized the need for effective defense systems to protect civilians from such brutal assaults. While local teams work tirelessly to rescue those trapped under rubble and attend to the wounded, the dire situation continues to unfold. The civilian infrastructure has been severely impacted, with power outages reported in several districts. This latest strike on Kyiv underscores the volatile and precarious nature of the conflict, leaving the city and its residents to grapple with the immediate aftermath and the looming threat of further aggression.
