'Most massive' Russian attack on Kyiv kills at least 18

The recent attack on Kyiv has been described as the 'most massive' assault on the Ukrainian capital since the conflict began. At least 18 people have lost their lives in what is a devastating blow to the city. Mayor Vitali Klitschko has declared a day of mourning, expressing solidarity with the bereaved families and calling for resilience in the face of adversity. The assault involved a barrage of drones and missiles aimed at strategic targets across the city, causing widespread destruction and panic among residents. Many buildings were damaged, and emergency services worked tirelessly to rescue survivors and provide medical aid to the injured. The international community has condemned the attack, with leaders urging for a peaceful resolution to the escalating conflict. In response, Ukraine has called for increased support from its allies, emphasizing the need for defensive measures to protect its cities and citizens. This attack underscores the urgent need for diplomatic efforts to prevent further escalation and loss of life. As the people of Kyiv mourn their losses, the world watches closely, hoping for an end to the violence that continues to ravage the region. The situation remains tense as authorities assess the damage and reinforce their defenses against future threats.
