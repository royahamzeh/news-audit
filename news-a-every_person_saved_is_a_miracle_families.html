'Every person saved is a miracle': Families call to trapped loved ones in region devastated by Venezuela quakes

In the aftermath of the devastating earthquakes that hit Venezuela, families continue to gather around collapsed structures, hoping for the safe rescue of their loved ones. Each successful rescue is considered nothing short of a miracle, as people are pulled from the ruins amidst overwhelming odds. The earthquakes have created a harrowing situation, leaving buildings reduced to rubble and streets filled with debris. In the face of such destruction, family members face the daunting challenge of moving massive debris by themselves, a task far beyond their capabilities. Volunteer groups and rescue teams are working tirelessly, but the scale of the disaster presents significant challenges. Emotions run high as families call out to their trapped relatives, clinging to hope with each moment that passes. Together with rescue workers, they listen intently, straining to hear any responsive sounds from those stuck beneath the heavy wreckage. Many families have taken the dire situation into their own hands, frantically working alongside rescue teams in a desperate bid to reach their trapped relatives. Yet, in these difficult circumstances, each person saved from the debris is not just a life spared but a beacon of hope, epitomizing what many describe as miracles amidst unimaginable adversity.
