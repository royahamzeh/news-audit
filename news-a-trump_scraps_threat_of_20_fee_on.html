Trump scraps threat of 20% fee on Hormuz cargo as US resumes blockade of Iran ports

In a surprising turn of events, former President Donald Trump has retracted his recent threat to impose a 20% fee on cargo ships passing through the Strait of Hormuz. This development comes just 24 hours after the initial announcement, which had sent ripples across the global shipping industry. President Trump's decision to scrap the fee appeared to be in response to mounting pressure from shipping companies and strategic allies who warned about potential disruptions in oil supply and international trade. The threat was initially part of a broader strategy to counter Iran's influence over the crucial waterway, through which nearly a fifth of the world's oil passes. However, the U.S. continues its aggressive stance by resuming its blockade of Iranian ports, a move likely intended to further isolate Iran economically and politically. This aggressive U.S. foreign policy is seen as an attempt to curtail Iran's regional power, although critics argue it could escalate tensions between the two nations and potentially involve other global powers. While the fee threat has been retracted, the situation remains tense as the U.S. maintains its pressure on Iran, making it clear that the battle over the Strait of Hormuz and the wider geopolitical dynamics are far from over.
