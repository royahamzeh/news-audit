US Supreme Court upholds birthright citizenship in blow to Trump

In a landmark decision, the United States Supreme Court upheld the principle of birthright citizenship, delivering a significant blow to former President Donald Trump's long-standing efforts to challenge this constitutional right. The court's ruling reaffirms the 14th Amendment, which guarantees citizenship to all individuals born on U.S. soil, regardless of their parents' nationality or immigration status. This decision marks a critical victory for civil rights advocates who have tirelessly fought to preserve this constitutional guarantee against attempts to erode it. The ruling effectively halts attempts that sought to limit or redefine the parameters of American citizenship, aligning with the judiciary's role in upholding fundamental rights enshrined in the Constitution. Opposition from Trump's camp viewed this as a missed opportunity to reshape immigration policies, while proponents see it as a necessary affirmation of a long-standing constitutional principle. Civil rights groups across the nation celebrated the verdict as it ensures equality and reaffirmation of the rights of millions of children born to immigrants in the U.S. The decision underscores the enduring strength of the American judicial system in protecting individual rights, maintaining a clear stance against efforts to undermine constitutional guarantees. Moving forward, this verdict solidifies the legal precedent concerning birthright citizenship, fostering a renewed sense of trust in the judicial system's role as a safeguard for civil liberties.
