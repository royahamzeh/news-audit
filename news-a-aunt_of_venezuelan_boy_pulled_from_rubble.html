Aunt of Venezuelan boy pulled from rubble tells BBC she will give him 'mother's warmth'

In a miraculous turn of events, a 2-year-old Venezuelan boy named Kleiber Moran was rescued from the rubble on Wednesday, after being trapped for six days following last week's devastating earthquakes. The catastrophic seismic event impacted the region heavily, leading to numerous buildings collapsing and causing widespread chaos. Rescue operations have been ongoing, but the successful extraction of Kleiber has brought renewed hope and determination to rescue workers and families affected by the disaster. The young boy's aunt, in an interview with the BBC, has expressed her deep relief and happiness at her nephew's survival. She stated her firm intention to provide Kleiber with a nurturing and loving environment, almost like a mother's, to help him recover from this traumatic ordeal. The aunt, whose name has not been disclosed, emphasizes the importance of familial love and support in aiding Kleiber's physical and emotional healing. Rescue teams have continued to scour the devastated areas, remaining hopeful that there may be others, like Kleiber, who are still clinging to life and awaiting salvation. The community, though shaken, finds solace in this story of survival amidst the catastrophe, representing the resilience of the human spirit even in dire circumstances.
