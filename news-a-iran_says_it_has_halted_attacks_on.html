Iran says it has halted attacks on Israel after first exchange of fire since truce

In a surprising turn of events, Iran announced that it has ceased its attacks on Israel after engaging in the first exchange of fire since a truce was established. Tensions escalated when Iran launched approximately 30 missiles at Israel following a strike in Lebanon. In retaliation, Israel conducted two waves of air strikes targeting Iranian facilities. The exchanges have raised concerns about the stability of the truce that had been maintaining a fragile peace in the region. The initial trigger for the escalation appears to be rooted in the complicated dynamics involving Lebanon, a country that often finds itself in the crosshairs of regional hostilities. The international community has called for restraint and dialogue to de-escalate the tensions, fearing that any prolonged conflict could lead to broader regional instability. Iran's cessation of hostilities has been welcomed as a step back from the brink, though the situation remains volatile. Analysts suggest that both countries are keen to avoid further escalation but are also wary of appearing weak. As diplomatic channels work overtime to mediate, the situation underscores the precarious balance of power in the Middle East, where alliances and enmities shift rapidly and have far-reaching consequences.
