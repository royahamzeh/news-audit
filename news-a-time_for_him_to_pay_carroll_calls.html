'Time for him to pay' - Carroll calls on Trump to pay $5m after president's appeal fails

E. Jean Carroll, the former Elle magazine columnist, has urged former President Donald Trump to comply with a jury's decision requiring him to pay $5 million in damages, following the rejection of his appeal. Carroll, who accused Trump of sexual abuse and defamation, emphasized that it's time for Trump to be held accountable for his actions. The jury's decision came after a long legal battle where Carroll claimed that Trump sexually assaulted her in a department store in the mid-1990s and subsequently defamed her character when she made her accusations public. Trump's legal team had filed an appeal attempting to overturn the verdict, which has been denied, leading to an outcry for justice and immediate payment of the damages. Despite the jury's ruling, Trump's attorneys are reportedly seeking ways to delay the payment through additional legal maneuvers. Carroll's legal team has expressed frustration with Trump's attempts to avoid financial responsibility, calling these efforts a continuation of his attempts to evade justice. This case marks a significant moment in the ongoing #MeToo movement, highlighting the challenges faced when holding powerful figures accountable after allegations of sexual misconduct emerge.
