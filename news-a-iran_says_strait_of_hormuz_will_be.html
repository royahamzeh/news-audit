Iran says Strait of Hormuz will be closed over Israel attacks on Lebanon

Iran has escalated tensions in the Middle East by threatening to close the strategic Strait of Hormuz in response to Israel's military actions in Lebanon. Tehran claims that these actions violate an existing agreement between Iran and the United States, which was aimed at ending the hostilities in the region. The Strait of Hormuz is a crucial maritime chokepoint through which a significant portion of the world's oil supply is transported. The potential closure of this waterway could have severe implications for global oil markets and contribute to heightened geopolitical instability. According to Iranian officials, Israel’s aggressive stance and recent military incursions into Lebanon represent a direct challenge to the terms of the peace agreement brokered with the US. By threatening to block the Strait of Hormuz, Iran is signaling its willingness to leverage its geographical influence over global oil routes as a means of exerting pressure. Analysts suggest that this move is also intended to rally domestic support by projecting strength against perceived external threats. However, the full extent of Iran's willingness to follow through on this threat remains uncertain, as does the international community's response to an action that could disrupt worldwide energy supplies.
